Painful parting

As dawn began to edge over the horizon, casting pale light through the curtains, they stood close but still—locked in a silence too full of words they couldn’t say. Their breath mingled, caught somewhere between passion and the unyielding weight of goodbye. Her hand lingered on his cheek, fingers tracing a line down to his lips as if memorizing their shape. He closed his eyes, pulling her closer by the waist, breathing in the familiar scent of her perfume, wanting to freeze the moment for one last second before everything slipped away.

Neither could find the right words; it was as if they all tangled in their throats. Instead, he leaned in, catching her mouth in a kiss that was both hungry and tender, deep enough to hold everything they'd felt but never spoken aloud. She responded in kind, her hands grabbing his hair, trying to hold on, knowing she’d have to let go.

The kiss ended, but they stayed close, foreheads touching, breaths mingling in the small space between them.

"Will I see you again?" she asked, her voice barely above a whisper, yet it resonated in the quiet room.

He hesitated, eyes dark with the unspoken answer. "We can’t keep this going. You know that."

Her fingers fell from his face, and she nodded, swallowing back tears. One last, lingering touch of her hand on his, and then she turned, crossing the room slowly as though hoping each step might erase the inevitability of this parting. She didn’t look back to the bed where they used to escape from the harsh reality for brief moments of selfish bliss. The door closed softly behind her, leaving him alone with her scent and the fading warmth of where she had been, the echo of her touch lingering like a bruise he’d carry for far too long.
